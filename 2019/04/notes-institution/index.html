Lemon Market: Continuous Quality

  • Quality of car: \(s\in [0,\bar{s}]\)
    • pdf: \(f(s) = α s^{α-1}\)
    • cdf: \(F(s) = s^α\)
  • Seller’s type: \(θ_1\)
    • Seller’s preferences: \(\begin{cases}p & \text{sell} \\ θ_1s & \text{not sell} \end{cases}\)
  • Buyer’s type: \(θ_2\)
    • Consumption possibilities: 1 or 0
    • Buyer’s preferences: \(\begin{cases}θ_2s - p & \text{buy}\\ 0 & \text{not buy}\end{cases}\)
  • Assume \(θ_2 > θ_1\)

Perfect Information

  • Trade is efficient since \[\begin{aligned}θ_2 &> θ_1 \\ θ_2s &> θ_1s \\ p + (θ_2s - p) &> θ_1s + 0\end{aligned}\]
  • Conditions for \(p\): \(\begin{cases}p \geq θ_1s & \text{(seller)} \\ θ_2s - p \geq 0 & \text{(buyer)}\end{cases}\) hold with \(θ_2s \geq p\geq θ_1s\)

Unknown Quality

  • Given price \(p\)
  • Seller sells if \[\begin{aligned}θ_1s \leq p \\ s\leq \frac{p}{θ_1} \end{aligned}\]
  • Buyer’s expected quality
    Let \(z = \frac{p}{θ_1}\), expected quality is the center of mass of \(f(s)\)
    \[\begin{aligned}E[s \ | \ p] &= E\Big[s \ \big| s\leq \frac{p}{θ_1}\Big] \\ &= E[s \ | \ s\leq z] \\ &= \frac{\int^z_0 s\cdot f(s)\, ds}{F(z)} \\ &= \frac{\int^z_0 s\cdot αs^{α-1}\, ds}{z^α} \\ &= α\cdot\frac{\int^z_0 s^α\, ds}{z^α} \\ &= α\cdot\frac{\frac{1}{α+1}s^{α+1}}{z^α} \bigg|_0^z \\ &= \frac{α}{α+1}\cdot z \\ &= \frac{α}{α+1}\cdot\frac{p}{θ_1}\end{aligned}\]
  • Buyer buys if \[\begin{aligned}θ_2\cdot E[s] - p &\geq 0 \\ θ_2\cdot\frac{α}{α+1}\cdot\frac{p}{θ_1} - p &\geq 0 \\ θ_2\cdot\frac{α}{α+1}\cdot\frac{1}{θ_1} &\geq 1 \\ θ_2 &\geq θ_1\cdot\frac{α+1}{α}\end{aligned}\]
  • Since \(\frac{α+1}{α}>1\), the market is smaller than efficiency

PS1.Q2
Agent 1 finds an old book in his basement and considers selling it on the flee market. Agent 1’s utility from keeping the book is \(θ_1s\), where \(s\) is the quality of the book.
On the flee market, agent 2 is potentially willing to buy the book. Agent 2’s utility from buying the book is \(θ_2s − p\) if he buys at price \(p\).
1. If \(s\) is publicly observed, when is trade effcient?
2. Suppose now agent 1 does not know the value of the book.
Agent 2 knows \(s\) but agent 1 knows only that \(s\in [0, 1]\) is equally likely.
Suppose that an auctioneer announces a price \(p\) at which trade can occur.
What expected value of quality can agent 1 infer from the fact that trade occurs at price \(p\)?

Competitive Equilibrium

  • \(s\in [\underline{s},\bar{s}]\)
  • \(N\) sellers, \(N\) buyers
  • Sellers’ type: \(θ_0\) (all same)
  • Buyers’ type: \(θ\)
    • pdf: \(g(θ)\)
    • cdf: \(G(θ)\), \(G(\hat{θ}) = Pr(θ\leq \hat{θ})\)
  • Perfect information
    For each seller, willingness to sell: \[p(s) = θ_0\cdot s\] For each buyer, willingness to buy: \[p(θ,s) = θ\cdot s\] Each buyer will trade with seller with car of quality \(s\) if \[\begin{aligned} p(θ,s) &\geq p(s) \\ θ\cdot s &\geq θ_0\cdot s \\ θ &\geq θ_0\end{aligned}\] Volume of trade: \[\begin{aligned} D &= N\cdot Pr(θ\geq θ_0) \\ &= N\cdot\big[ 1 - G(θ_0) \big]\end{aligned}\]
  • Asymmetric information
    Given price \(p\), sellers sell if \[\begin{aligned}p &\geq θ_0\cdot s \\ s &\leq \frac{p}{θ_0} \\ S(p) &= N\cdot Pr\Big(s\leq \frac{p}{θ_0}\Big) \\ &= N\cdot F\Big( \frac{p}{θ_0}\Big)\end{aligned}\] Buyers’ expected quality: \[s^e(p) := E\Big[ s \ \big| \ s\leq \frac{p}{θ_0}\Big]\] Buyers buy if \[\begin{aligned} θ\cdot s^e(p) &\geq p \\ θ &\geq \frac{p}{s^e(p)} \\ D(p) &= N\cdot Pr\Big(θ \geq \frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big) \\ &= N\Big[ 1 - G\Big(\frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big) \Big]\end{aligned}\] Market clears when \[\begin{aligned}S(p) &= D(p) \\ N\cdot F\Big( \frac{p}{θ_0}\Big) &= N\Big[ 1 - G\Big(\frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big) \Big] \\ F\Big( \frac{p}{θ_0}\Big) &= 1 - G\Big(\frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big) \end{aligned}\]
  • Locally increasing demand exists when \[\begin{aligned}D'(p) = -N\cdot\underbrace{g\Big(\frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big)}_{>0}\cdot\frac{\partial}{\partial p} \Big(\frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big) & > 0 \\ -\frac{\partial}{\partial p} \Big(\frac{p}{s^e(p)}\Big) &> 0 \\ \frac{s^e(p) - \frac{\partial s^e(p)}{\partial p}\cdot p}{s^e(p)^2} &< 0 \\ s^e(p) - \frac{\partial s^e(p)}{\partial p}\cdot p &< 0 \\ \frac{\partial s^e(p)}{\partial p}\cdot\frac{p}{s^e(p)} &> 1 \\ \frac{\partial s^e(p) / s^e(p)}{\partial p / p} &> 1 \\ \text{Elasticity of expected quality} &> 1\end{aligned}\]

Lemon Market: Binary Quality

  • Quality of car: \(s\in\{\underline{s},\bar{s}\}\)
    • Probability that a car is \(\bar{s}\): \(α\)
    • Probability that \(\bar{s}\) does not need repair: \(\bar{s} = r\cdot 1 + (1-r)\cdot 0 = r\)
    • Probability that \(\underline{s}\) does not need repair: \(\underline{s} = q\cdot 1 + (1-q)\cdot 0 = q\)
    • \(r > q\)
  • Seller’s type: \(θ_1\)
    • Seller’s preferences: \(\begin{cases}p &\text{sell} \\ θ_1\cdot 1 - (1-s)\cdot c &\text{repair} \\ s\cdot θ_1 &\text{not repair} \end{cases}\)
  • Buyers’ type: \(θ_2\), 2 buyers
    • Buyers’ preferences: \(\begin{cases}θ_2\cdot 1 - (1-s)\cdot c - p &\text{repair} \\ θ_2\cdot s - p &\text{not repair} \\ 0 &\text{not buy} \end{cases}\)
  • Assume \(θ_2 > θ_1\)
  • Repair cost: \(c\)

Cheap Repair

  • \(c \leq θ_1 < θ_2\)
    • Seller will repair if doesn’t sell: \[\begin{aligned}θ_1 - (1-s)\cdot c &\geq s\cdot θ_1 \\ θ_1 - c + s\cdot c &\geq s\cdot θ_1 \\ (s-1)\cdot c &\geq (s-1)\cdot θ_1 && (s-1<0) \\ c &\leq θ_1\end{aligned}\]
  • Seller sells if \[θ_1 - (1-s)\cdot c\leq p\]
  • Buyers’ expected quality: \[\begin{aligned} E[s] &= αr + (1-α)q \\ 1 - E[s] &= 1 - \big[αr + (1-α)q\big] \\ &= 1 - αr - q + αq \\ &= 1 - αr - q + αq + α - α\\ &= α(1-r) + (1-α)(1-q)\end{aligned}\] Buyers buy if \[\begin{aligned}θ_2\cdot 1 - \big(1-E[s]\big)\cdot c - p &\geq 0 \\ θ_2 - \big[α(1-r) + (1-α)(1-q)\big]\cdot c &\geq p\end{aligned}\]
  • Price for \(\bar{s}\) exists if \[\begin{aligned}θ_2 - \big[α(1-r) + (1-α)(1-q)\big]\cdot c &\geq θ_1 - (1-r)\cdot c \\ θ_2 &\geq θ_1 + \big[α(1-r) + (1-α)(1-q)\big]\cdot c - (1-r)\cdot c \\ &\geq θ_1 + (1-α)(r-q)\cdot c\end{aligned}\]
  • Since \((1-α)(r-q)\cdot c > 0\), the market is smaller than efficiency

Expensive Repair

  • \(c \geq θ_2 > θ_1\)
    • Seller will not repair if doesn’t sell: \(c \geq θ_1\)
    • Buyer will not repair if the car needs repair: \[\begin{aligned}θ_2\cdot 1 - (1-s)\cdot c - p &\leq θ_2\cdot s - p \\ θ_2\cdot 1 - (1-s)\cdot c &\leq θ_2\cdot s \\ (1-s)\cdot θ_2 &\leq (1-s)\cdot c \\ θ_2 &\leq c\end{aligned}\]
  • Seller sells if \[s\cdot θ_1 \leq p\]
  • Buyers buy if \[\begin{aligned}θ_2\cdot E[s] - p &\geq 0 \\ θ_2\cdot \big[αr + (1-α)q\big] &\geq p \end{aligned}\]
  • Price for \(\bar{s}\) exists if \[\begin{aligned}θ_2\cdot \big[αr + (1-α)q\big] &\geq r\cdot θ_1 \\ θ_2 &\geq \frac{r\cdot θ_1}{αr + (1-α)q} \end{aligned}\]
  • Since \(\frac{r}{αr + (1-α)q} > 1\), the market is smaller than efficiency

Lemon Market: Signalling

Timing

  1. Nature chooses quality \(s\)
  2. Seller chooses whether to issue warranty \(w\)
  3. Buyers propose \(p\)
  4. Seller chooses to accept buyer 1, buyer 2 or nobody
  5. (If applicable) repair

Strategy

  • Seller’s strategy
    • Warranty: \(w(s): \{\underline{s},\bar{s}\} \to \{0,1\}\)
    • Acceptance: \(a(s,w,p_1,p_2): \{\underline{s},\bar{s}\}\times\{0,1\}\times\mathbb{R}^2 \to \{0,1,2\}\)
  • Buyers’ strategy: \(p(w): \{0,1\}\to\mathbb{R}\)
  • Buyers’ belief: \(μ(w): \{0,1\}\to [0,1]\)

Separating Equilibrium

  • Assumptions
    • Expensive repair: \(c \geq θ_2 > θ_1\)
    • Price for \(\bar{s}\) does not exist \[θ_2 < \frac{r\cdot θ_1}{αr + (1-α)q}\]
  • For \(\bar{s}\) seller, \[\begin{aligned}(IC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-r) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/ warranty}} &\geq \underbrace{q\cdot θ_2}_{\text{Sell w/o warranty}} \\ \implies && c &\leq \frac{1-q}{1-r}θ_2 \\ (PC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-r) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/ warranty}} &\geq \underbrace{r\cdot θ_1}_{\text{Not sell}} \\ \implies && c &\leq \frac{θ_2 - r\cdot θ_1}{1-r}\end{aligned}\]
  • For \(\underline{s}\) seller, \[\begin{aligned}(IC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-q) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/ warranty}} &\leq \underbrace{q\cdot θ_2}_{\text{Sell w/o warranty}} \\ \implies && c&\geq θ_2 \\ (PC) && \underbrace{q\cdot θ_2}_{\text{Sell w/o warranty}} &\geq \underbrace{q\cdot θ_1}_{\text{Not sell}} \\ \implies && θ_2 &\geq θ_1\end{aligned}\]
  • Equilibrium when \(c\leq \frac{θ_2 - r\cdot θ_1}{1-r}\)
    \(\begin{cases} w(\bar{s}) = 1 \\ w(\underline{s}) = 0\end{cases}\), \(\begin{cases}μ(1) = 1 \\ μ(0) = 0 \end{cases}\), \(\begin{cases}p(1) = θ_2 \\ p(0) = q\cdot θ_2 \end{cases}\)
  • When \(c> \frac{θ_2 - r\cdot θ_1}{1-r}\), \(\bar{s}\) seller never sells

  2. Pooling equilibrium with no warranty does not exist because \(\bar{s}\) seller will deviate by issuing warranty
  3. Separating equilibrium
    Buyers’ belief: \(\begin{cases}μ(1) = r \\ μ(0) = q \end{cases}\)
    Buyers’ willingness to buy: \(p = \begin{cases} θ_2 & w=1 \\ θ_2 - (1-q)\cdot c & w=0\end{cases}\)
    For \(\bar{s}\) seller, \[\begin{aligned}(IC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-r) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/ warranty}} &\geq \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-q)\cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/o warranty}} \\ \implies && r &\geq q \\ (PC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-r) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/ warranty}} &\geq \underbrace{θ_1 - (1-r) \cdot c}_{\text{Not sell}} \\ \implies && θ_2&\geq θ_1\end{aligned}\] For \(\underline{s}\) seller, \[\begin{aligned}(IC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-q) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/ warranty}} &\leq \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-q)\cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/o warranty}} \\ \implies && q &\geq q \\ (PC) && \underbrace{θ_2 - (1-q) \cdot c}_{\text{Sell w/o warranty}} &\geq \underbrace{θ_1 - (1-q) \cdot c}_{\text{Not sell}} \\ \implies && θ_2 &\geq θ_1\end{aligned}\]
  4. There is no equilibrium where a \(\bar{s}\) seller does not sell

Adverse Selection (MWG Ch 13)

also see #Lemon Market Competitive Equilibrium

  • Type of workers: \(θ\in [\underline{θ},\bar{θ}]\)
    • pdf: \(f(θ)\)
    • cdf: \(F(θ)\), \(F(\hat{θ}) = Pr(θ\leq \hat{θ})\)
  • \(N\) workers, many firms
  • Reservation utility: \(r(θ)\geq 0\)
  • Wage: \(w(θ)\)

Benchmark

  • Existence of wage
    Worker \(θ\) will accept job if \(w(θ) \geq r(θ)\)
    Firms will employ worker \(θ\) if \(w(θ)\leq θ\)
    Worker \(θ\) will be employed if \[\begin{aligned}r(θ)&\leq w(θ)\leq θ \\ r(θ) &\leq θ \\ w^*(θ) &= θ\end{aligned}\]
  • Social planner’s problem
    Let \(I(θ) =\begin{cases}0 &\text{not employed} \\ 1&\text{employed} \end{cases}\), \[\begin{aligned}\max_{I(θ)} && &\phantom{\ggg}\int_{\underline{θ}}^{\bar{θ}} N\cdot\Big[ I(θ)\cdot θ + \big(1-I(θ)\big)\cdot r(θ) \Big] \,dF(θ) \\ && &= \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\bar{θ}} N\cdot\Big[ I(θ)\cdot θ + \big(1-I(θ)\big)\cdot r(θ) \Big]\cdot f(θ) \,dθ \\ && &I^*(θ) = \begin{cases}0 & θ < r(θ) \\ 1 & θ \geq r(θ) \end{cases}\end{aligned}\]
  • Number of employed workers: \(N\cdot\big[1 - F\big(r(θ)\big)\big]\)

Unknown Type Competitive Equilibrium

  • Assumptions \(\implies E[θ]< r(\bar{θ})\) (adverse selection)
    1. \(r(θ)\leq θ\) for all \(θ\) (efficient for all workers to be employed)
    2. \(1 < r'(θ) < 0\)
  • Wage: (given) \(w\) for all \(θ\)
  • Set of workers accept \(w\): \(Θ(w) = \{θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w\}\)
  • Firms’ belief: \(μ(w) = E[θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w]\)
    • Off-track belief: \(μ = E[θ]\) when \(Θ = \emptyset\)
  • Firms’ demand: \(z(w) = \begin{cases}0 & μ<w \\ [0, \infty) & μ= w \\\infty & μ>w\end{cases}\)
  • Equilibrium \[\begin{aligned}\text{Workers' BR} && Θ^* &= \{θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w^*\} \\ \text{Firms' BR} && w^* &= E[θ \ | \ θ\in Θ^*] \\ && &= E[θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w^*]\end{aligned}\]
  • Adverse selection arises in step 4 & 5
    1. When \(w<r(θ)\), no worker accept
    2. When \(w=r(\underline{θ})\), only worker \(\underline{θ}\) accepts
    3. In equilibrium, \(E[θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w^*] = w^*\)
    4. When \(w=r(\bar{θ})\), all workers accept, \(E[θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w] = E[θ]\)
    5. When \(w>r(\bar{θ})\), since \(E[θ] = \text{sup} \ E[θ \ | \ r(θ)\leq w]\), the function stays constant

Constrained Pareto Optimum

  • Government (social planner) intervention
    • Wage: \(w_e\)
    • Unemployment benefit: \(w_u\)
    • Set of workers accept \(w_e\): \(Θ = \{θ \ | \ w_u+r(θ)\leq w_e\}\)
  • Assume social planner wants to let \(Θ\leq\hat{θ}\) accept the job
    \[\begin{aligned}\text{Marginal type} && &w_u + r(\hat{θ}) = w_e \\ \text{Budget balance} && &\underbrace{w_e\cdot F(\hat{θ})}_{\text{Total wage}} + \underbrace{w_u\cdot\big(1 - F(\hat{θ})\big)}_{\text{Total unemployment benefit}} = \underbrace{\int_θ^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ}_{\text{Total productivity}}\end{aligned}\] solving the system gives
    \[\begin{aligned}\big(w_u + r(\hat{θ})\big)&\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + w_u\cdot\big(1 - F(\hat{θ})\big) = \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ w_u(\hat{θ}) &= - r(\hat{θ})\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ &= - r(\hat{θ})\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + F(\hat{θ})\cdot E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}]\\ &= F(\hat{θ})\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}] - r(\hat{θ})\big) \\ w_e(\hat{θ}) &= r(\hat{θ})\cdot\big(1- F(\hat{θ})\big) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ &= F(\hat{θ})\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}] - r(\hat{θ})\big) + r(\hat{θ})\end{aligned}\]
  • When \(\hat{θ} = θ^*\) in #Unknown Type Competitive Equilibrium
    \[\begin{aligned}w_u(θ^*) &= F(θ^*)\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq θ^*] - r(θ^*)\big) \\ &= F(θ^*)\cdot 0 = 0 \\ w_e(θ^*) &= w_u(θ^*) + r(θ^*) \\ &= r(θ^*) = w^*\end{aligned}\]
  • When \(\hat{θ} < θ^*\) \[\begin{aligned}r(\hat{θ}) &< r(θ^*) \\ w_e(\hat{θ}) &= r(\hat{θ})\cdot\big(\overbrace{1- F(\hat{θ})}^{>0}\big) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ &< r(θ^*)\cdot\big(1- F(\hat{θ})\big) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ w_e(\hat{θ}) - r(θ^*) &< -r(θ^*)\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ &= - r(θ^*)\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + F(\hat{θ})\cdot E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}]\\ &= F(\hat{θ})\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}] - r(θ^*)\big) \\ &= F(\hat{θ})\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}] - E[θ \ | \ θ\leq θ^*]\big) \\ &< 0 \\ w_e(\hat{θ}) &< r(θ^*) \\ &= w_e(θ^*)\end{aligned}\] Therefore, workers who work both before and after government intervention are strictly worse-off
  • When \(\hat{θ} > θ^*\)
    \[\begin{aligned}r(\hat{θ}) &> r(θ^*) \\ w_u(\hat{θ}) &= -r(\hat{θ})\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ &< -r(θ^*)\cdot \cdot F(\hat{θ}) + \int_{\underline{θ}}^{\hat{θ}} θ\cdot f(θ)\, dθ \\ &= -r(θ^*)\cdot F(\hat{θ}) + F(\hat{θ})\cdot E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}]\\ &= F(\hat{θ})\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}] - r(θ^*)\big) \\ &= F(\hat{θ})\cdot \big(E[θ \ | \ θ\leq\hat{θ}] - E[θ \ | \ θ\leq θ^*]\big) \\ &< 0 = w_u(θ^*)\\ w_u(\hat{θ}) &< w_u(θ^*)\end{aligned}\] Therefore, workers who don’t work both before and after government intervention are strictly worse-off

Moral Hazard (Hidden Action)

  • Return: \(v\)
  • Effort level: \(e\)
    • Technology: \(F(\hat{v} \ | \ e) = Pr(v\leq \hat{v} \ | \ e)\)
    • Cost: \(c(e)\)
  • Contract: \(t\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(v - t\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(t - c(e)\)
  • Reservation utility: \(\underline{U} = 0\)
  • Incentive compatibility constraint (IC): agent chooses the level of effort that maximizes their own utility
  • Participation constraint (PC): agent accepts the contract only if the expected utility exceeds other alternatives (their reservation utility)

Timing

  1. Principal offers contract: \(t\)
  2. Agent accepts or rejects
  3. Agent chooses \(e\) (unobservable)
  4. \(v\) is realized (verifiable)
  5. Payment \(t\) is made

Benchmark

  • First best solution: \[\begin{aligned}e^{FB} &= \text{argmax} \ E[v \ | \ e] - c(e) \\ &= \begin{cases} p(e)\cdot v_H + \big( 1-p(e)\big) \cdot v_L & v\in\{ v_L, v_H\} \\ \int_{v_L}^{v_H} v\cdot f(v|e)dv & v\in [v_L, v_H]\end{cases}\end{aligned}\] where \(p(e) = Pr(v = v_H \ | \ e)\)
  • Franchising contract: \(t(v) = v-a\) (may be positive or negative)
    • Agent’s problem \[\begin{aligned}\max_e && E[t(v) \ | \ e] - c(e) &= E[v \ | \ e] - a - c(e) \\ && e^* &= e^{FB} \end{aligned}\]
    • Principal’s payoff \[\begin{aligned}v-t(v) &= a \\ &= E[v \ | \ e^{FB}] - c(e^{FB})\end{aligned}\]

Exercise I
A principal can make a take-it-or-leave-it contract offer to an agent.

Adverse Selection

One-Sided Timing

First Best

  • Return: \(v\)
    • \(v'(x)>0\), \(v''(x)<0\)
    • \(v'(0) = \infty\), \(\lim_{x\to\infty} \ v'(x) = 0\)
  • Agent’s cost: \(c\in\{ c_L, c_H\}\)
    • Probability: \(Pr(c= c_L) = p\)
    • Reservation utility: \(\underline{U} = 0\)
  • Contract: \(t\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(v(x) - t\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(t - c\cdot x\)

    • Principal’s strategy: \(s_p\in S_p\)
    • Agent’s strategy: \(s_a(c)\in S_a\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(v\Big(X\big(s_a(c), s_p\big)\Big) - T\big(s_a(c), s_p\big)\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(T\big(s_a(c), s_p\big) - c\cdot X\big(s_a(c), s_p\big)\)
    • Equilibrium \[\begin{aligned}s_p^* &= \text{argmax}_{s_p} \ v\Big(X\big(s_a^*(c), s_p\big)\Big) - T\big(s_a^*(c), s_p\big) \\ s_a^* &= \text{argmax}_{s_a} \ T\big(s_a(c), s_p^*\big) - c\cdot X\big(s_a(c), s_p^*\big)\end{aligned}\]
  • Game induced by direct mechanism
    By revelation principle, implementable = truthfully implementable
    • Principal’s strategy: \(t(c) = T\big(s_a^*(c), s_p^*\big)\)
    • Agent’s strategy: \(x(c) = X\big(s_a^*(c), s_p^*\big)\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(v\big(x(c)\big) - t(c)\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(t(c) - c\cdot x(c)\)

Second Best

  • Contract: \(\begin{cases}x_L = x(c_L) \\ t_L = t(c_L)\end{cases}\), \(\begin{cases}x_H = x(c_H) \\ t_H = t(c_H)\end{cases}\)
  • Principal’s problem \[\begin{aligned}\max_{x_L, x_H, t_L, t_H} && p\cdot\big( v(x_L) - t_L\big) &+ (1-p)\cdot\big( v(x_H) - t_H\big) \\ (IC_L) && t_L - c_L\cdot x_L&\geq t_H - c_L\cdot x_H\\ \implies && t_L - c_L\cdot x_L&= t_H - c_L\cdot x_H \\ (IC_H) && t_H - c_H\cdot x_H&\geq t_L - c_H\cdot x_L\\ (PC_L) && t_L - c_L\cdot x_L&\geq \underline{U} = 0\\ (PC_H) && t_H - c_H\cdot x_H&\geq \underline{U} = 0 \\ \implies && t_H - c_H\cdot x_H &= 0\end{aligned}\] Therefore, \[\begin{aligned}t_H &= c_H\cdot x_H \\ t_L &= c_L\cdot x_L + t_H - c_L\cdot x_H \\ &= c_L\cdot x_L + c_H\cdot x_H - c_L\cdot x_H \\ &= c_L\cdot x_L + \underbrace{x_H\cdot( c_H - c_L)}_{\text{Information rent}}\end{aligned}\] Plug back into Principal’s problem \[\begin{aligned}\max_{x_L, x_H} && p&\cdot\big( v(x_L) - c_L\cdot x_L - x_H\cdot( c_H - c_L)\big) \\ && &+ (1-p)\cdot\big( v(x_H) - c_H\cdot x_H) \\ FOC && \frac{\partial}{\partial x_L} &= p\cdot\big( v'(x_L) -c_L\big) \\ && v'(x_L^{SB}) &= c_L \\ && x_L^{SB} &= x^{FB}(c_L)\implies\text{No distortion at the top}\\ && \frac{\partial}{\partial x_H} &= -p\cdot (c_H - c_L) + (1- p)\cdot\big( v'(x_H) -\cdot c_H) \\ && v'(x_H^{SB}) &= c_H + \frac{p}{1-p}\cdot(c_H - c_L) \\ && x_H^{SB} &< x^{FB}(c_H)\implies\text{Downward distortion}\end{aligned}\] Check \((IC_H)\) \[\begin{aligned}t_H - c_H\cdot x_H &= c_H\cdot x_H - c_H\cdot x_H = 0 \\ t_L - c_H\cdot x_L &= c_L\cdot x_L + x_H\cdot( c_H - c_L) - c_H\cdot x_L \\ &= x_H\cdot( c_H - c_L) - x_L\cdot( c_H - c_L) \\ &= (x_H - x_L)( c_H - c_L) < 0 \\ \implies t_H - c_H\cdot x_H&< t_L - c_H\cdot x_L\end{aligned}\]

Principal’s problem: \[\begin{aligned}\max_{x,t} && &\phantom{\ggg}E[\sqrt{x} - t(x)] \\ && &= \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_L} - t_L) + \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_H} - t_H) \\ (IC_L) && &t_L - x_L\cdot c_L - F\geq t_H - x_H\cdot c_L - F \\ (PC_L) && &t_L - x_L\cdot c_L - F\geq 0 \\ (IC_H) && &t_H - x_H\cdot c_H - F\geq t_L - x_L\cdot c_H - F \\ (PC_H) && &t_H - x_H\cdot c_H - F\geq 0\end{aligned}\]
  3. \((PC_L)\) is redundant
    \((PC_H)\implies t_H = F + x_H\cdot c_H\)
    \((IC_L)\implies\) \[\begin{aligned}t_L &= t_H - x_H\cdot c_L + x_L\cdot c_L \\ &= F + x_H\cdot c_H - x_H\cdot c_L + x_L\cdot c_L \\ &= F + x_L\cdot c_L + \underbrace{x_H\cdot( c_H -c_L)}_{\text{Information rent}}\end{aligned}\] \((IC_H)\implies\) \[\begin{aligned} 0 &\geq (x_H -x_L)(c_H-c_L) \\ x_H -x_L &\leq 0 \\ x_H &\leq x_L\end{aligned}\] Plug back to principal’s problem: \[\begin{aligned}\max_x && \frac{1}{2}\big(\sqrt{x_L} - F - x_L\cdot c_L - x_H\cdot( c_H -c_L)\big) &+ \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_H} - F - x_H\cdot c_H) \\ FOC && \frac{1}{2}\Big(\frac{1}{2}x_L^{-\frac{1}{2}}-c_L\Big) &= 0 \\ && x_L^{SB} &= \frac{1}{4c_L^2} \\ && &= 1 = x_L^{FB}\\ && \frac{1}{2}\Big(-(c_H-c_H)+\frac{1}{2}x_H^{-\frac{1}{2}}-c_H\Big) &= 0 \\ && x_H^{SB} &= \frac{1}{(4c_H-2c_L)^2} \\ && &= \frac{1}{9} < x_H^{FB}\end{aligned}\] Therefore, \[\begin{aligned}t_H &= F + x_H\cdot c_H \\ &= F + \frac{1}{9} \\ t_L &= F + x_L\cdot c_L + x_H\cdot( c_H -c_L) \\ &= F + \frac{5}{9}\end{aligned}\] Principal’s expected profit: \[\begin{aligned}&\phantom{\ggg} \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_L} - t_L) + \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_H} - t_H) \\ &= \frac{1}{3} - F\end{aligned}\]
  4. Principal’s problem: \[\begin{aligned}\max_{x,t} && \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_L} - t_L)& \\ (PC_L) && t_L - x_L\cdot c_L - F &\geq 0 \\ (PC_H) && t_H - x_H\cdot c_H - F &< 0 \end{aligned}\] Therefore, \(t_L = F + x_L\cdot c_L\)
    Plug back to principal’s problem
    \[\begin{aligned}\max_x && \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_L} - F - x_L\cdot c_L)& \\ FOC && \frac{1}{2}\Big(\frac{1}{2}x_L^{-\frac{1}{2}}-c_L\Big) &= 0 \\ && x_L^{SB} &= \frac{1}{4c_L^2} \\ && &= 1\end{aligned}\] \[\begin{aligned}t_L &= F + x_L\cdot c_L \\ &= F + \frac{1}{2} \end{aligned}\] Principal’s expected profit: \[\begin{aligned}&\phantom{\ggg} \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{x_L} - t_L) \\ &= \frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{2}F\end{aligned}\] Therefore, principal dispenses with the participation of high type if \[\begin{aligned}\frac{1}{4} - \frac{1}{2}F &\geq \frac{1}{3} - F\\ F &\geq \frac{1}{6} \end{aligned}\]

Two-Sided Timing

  1. Agent learns their type \(c\), principal learns their type \(v\)
  2. Principal offers contract \(t\)
  3. Agent accepts or rejects
  4. Agent chooses quantity \(x\) (verifiable)
  5. Payment \(t\) is made

Benchmark

  • Principal’s type: \(v\)
    • cdf: \(G(v)\)
  • Agent’s cost: \(c\)
    • cdf: \(F(c)\)
    • Reservation utility: \(\underline{U} = 0\)
  • Contract: \(t\)
    • Quantity: \(x\in\{0,1\}\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(v\cdot x - t\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(t - c\cdot x\)
  • First-best solution \[\begin{aligned} x^{FB}(c,v) &= \text{argmax} \ v\cdot x - c\cdot x \\ &= \begin{cases}1 & v\geq c \\ 0 & v\leq c \end{cases}\end{aligned}\]
  • Game induced by mechanism
    • Principal’s strategy: \(s_p(v)\in S_p\)
    • Agent’s strategy: \(s_a(c)\in S_a\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(v\cdot X\big(s_a(c), s_p(v)\big) - T\big(s_a(c), s_p(v)\big)\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(T\big(s_a(c), s_p(v)\big) - c\cdot X\big(s_a(c), s_p(v)\big)\)
    • Bayesian equilibrium \[\begin{aligned}s_p^*(v) &= \text{argmax}_{s_p} \ E_c\Big[ v\cdot X\big(s_a^*(c), s_p\big) - T\big(s_a^*(c), s_p\big)\Big] \\ s_a^*(c) &= \text{argmax}_{s_a} \ E_v\Big[ T\big(s_a, s_p^*(v)\big) - c\cdot X\big(s_a, s_p^*(v)\big)\Big]\end{aligned}\]
  • Game induced by direct mechanism
    • Principal’s strategy: \(t(c,v) = T\big(s_a^*(c), s_p^*(v)\big)\)
    • Agent’s strategy: \(x(c,v) = X\big(s_a^*(c), s_p^*(v)\big)\)
    • Principal’s payoff: \(E_c\big[ v\cdot x(c,v) - t(c,v)\big]\)
    • Agent’s payoff: \(E_v\big[ t(c,v) - c\cdot x(c,v)\big]\)

Exercise 5
A seller and a buyer can make a verifiable decision \(x\in \{0,1\}\), where \(x = 1\) means that a good is exchanged.
When the buyer makes a transfer payment \(t\) to the seller, then the parties’ payoffs are: \[\begin{aligned}\text{Buyer} && x\cdot v - t \\ \text{Seller} && t - x\cdot c\end{aligned}\] The cost \(c\in\{c_L,c_H\}\) is private information of the seller, the valuation \(v\in\{v_L,v_H\}\) is private information of the buyer.
It is commonly known that \(c\) and \(v\) are independently distributed random variables with \(Pr(c = c_L) = Pr(v = v_H) = \frac{1}{3}\) and \(0 < c_L < v_L < v_H < c_H\).
The parties’ reservation utilities are zero.
1. What is the ex post efficient decision rule, which maximizes the parties’ total surplus (first- best solution)?
From an ex ante point of view (i.e., before the random variables are realized), what is the expected total surplus when the ex post efficient decision rule will be applied?
2. What are the four incentive compatibility constraints that a direct mechanism must satisfy when the ex post efficient decision rule is to be implemented?
3. Suppose the parties are privately informed at the contracting stage already (“adverse selection”).
Please provide all participation constraints.
Can the ex post efficient decision rule be implemented when bargaining over the contract is voluntary?
If so, provide a direct mechanism that satisfies all incentive compatibility and participation constraints.
4. Suppose the parties learn their private information only after a contract has been signed (“hidden information”).
What are the participation constraints?
Can the ex post efficient decision rule be implemented when bargaining over the contract is voluntary?

  1. Social planner’s problem: \[\begin{aligned}\max_x && &x\cdot v - x\cdot c \\ && &x^{FB}(c_L) = 1\\ && &x^{FB}(c_H) = 0 \end{aligned}\] Total surplus: \[\begin{aligned}E^{FB}[x\cdot v - x\cdot c] &= \frac{2}{9}\cdot (v_L - c_L) + \frac{1}{9}\cdot(v_H - c_L) + \frac{6}{9}\cdot 0\\ &= \frac{2}{9}\cdot v_L + \frac{1}{9}\cdot v_H + \frac{1}{3}\cdot c_L\end{aligned}\]
  2. Direct mechanism: \(\big( t(c,v), x^{FB}(c,v)\big)\)
    Let \(t_{ij} = t(c_i, v_j)\),
    Incentive compatibility constraints for seller: \[\begin{aligned} (IC) c_L && E_v\big[ t(c_L,v) - c_L\cdot x^{FB}(c_L,v) \big] &\geq E_v\big[ t(c_H,v) - c_L\cdot x^{FB}(c_H,v) \big] \\ && \frac{1}{3} (t_{LH} - c_L) + \frac{2}{3} (t_{LL} - c_L) &\geq \frac{1}{3}(t_{HH} - 0\cdot c_L) + \frac{2}{3}(t_{HL} - 0\cdot c_L)\\ (IC) c_H && E_v\big[ t(c_H,v) - c_H\cdot x^{FB}(c_H,v) \big] &\geq E_v\big[ t(c_L,v) - c_H\cdot x^{FB}(c_L,v) \big] \\ && \frac{1}{3} (t_{HH} - 0\cdot c_H) + \frac{2}{3} (t_{HL} - 0\cdot c_H) &\geq \frac{1}{3}(t_{LH} - c_H) + \frac{2}{3}(t_{LL} - c_H) \end{aligned}\] For buyer: \[\begin{aligned} (IC) v_L && E_c\big[v_L\cdot x^{FB}(c,v_L) - t(c, v_L)\big] &\geq E_c\big[v_L\cdot x^{FB}(c,v_H) - t(c, v_H)\big] \\ && \frac{1}{3}(v_L - t_{LL}) + \frac{2}{3}(0\cdot v_L - t_{HL}) &\geq \frac{1}{3}(v_L - t_{LH}) + \frac{2}{3}(0\cdot v_L - t_{HH}) \\ (IC) v_H && E_c\big[v_H\cdot x^{FB}(c,v_H) - t(c, v_H)\big] &\geq E_c\big[v_H\cdot x^{FB}(c,v_L) - t(c, v_L)\big] \\ && \frac{1}{3}(v_H - t_{LH}) + \frac{2}{3}(0\cdot v_H - t_{HH}) &\geq \frac{1}{3}(v_H - t_{LL}) + \frac{2}{3}(0\cdot v_H - t_{HL})\end{aligned}\]
  3. Participate constraints for seller: \[\begin{aligned} (PC) c_L && E_v\big[ t(c_L,v) - c_L\cdot x^{FB}(c_L,v) \big] &\geq 0 \\ && \frac{1}{3} (t_{LH} - c_L) + \frac{2}{3} (t_{LL} - c_L) &\geq 0 \\ (PC) c_H && E_v\big[ t(c_H,v) - c_H\cdot x^{FB}(c_H,v) \big] &\geq 0 \\ && \frac{1}{3} (t_{HH} - 0\cdot c_H) + \frac{2}{3} (t_{HL} - 0\cdot c_H) &\geq 0\end{aligned}\] For buyer: \[\begin{aligned} (PC) v_L && E_c\big[v_L\cdot x^{FB}(c,v_L) - t(c, v_L)\big] &\geq 0 \\ && \frac{1}{3}(v_L - t_{LL}) + \frac{2}{3}(0\cdot v_L - t_{HL}) &\geq 0 \\ (PC) v_H && E_c\big[v_H\cdot x^{FB}(c,v_H) - t(c, v_H)\big] &\geq 0 \\ && \frac{1}{3}(v_H - t_{LH}) + \frac{2}{3}(0\cdot v_H - t_{HH}) &\geq \end{aligned}\] It is possible to satisfy all constraints simultaneously, for example: \[\begin{aligned} &\begin{cases}x^{FB}(c_L) = 1\\x^{FB}(c_H) = 0 \end{cases} \\ &\begin{cases}t_{LH} = t_{LL} = p\\ t_{HH} = t_{HL} = 0 \end{cases} \\ &p \in( c_L, v_L)\end{aligned}\]
  4. Participate constraints for seller: \[\begin{aligned} E_v\big[ t(c,v) - c\cdot x^{FB}(c,v) \big] &\geq 0 \\ \frac{1}{9} (t_{LH} - c_L) + \frac{2}{9} (t_{LL} - c_L) +\frac{2}{9} (t_{HH} - 0\cdot c_H) + \frac{4}{9} (t_{HL} - 0\cdot c_H) &\geq 0 \end{aligned}\] For buyer: \[\begin{aligned} E_c\big[v\cdot x^{FB}(c,v) - t(c, v)\big] &\geq 0 \\ \frac{1}{9}(v_H - t_{LH}) + \frac{2}{9}(v_L - t_{LL}) + \frac{2}{9}(0\cdot v_H - t_{HH})+ \frac{4}{9}(0\cdot v_H - t_{HL}) &\geq 0\end{aligned}\] It is possible to satisfy all constraints simultaneously with the mechanism and price provided in (3).
