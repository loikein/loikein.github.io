Relation

2014.F1.Q2
Let \(R\) be a relation on some set \(U\)
Assume that there exist two functions \(f,g: U\to \mathbb{R}\)
For all \(x,y\in U\), \(xRy\) \(\iff\) \(f(x)\geq g(y)\)
For all \(x\in U\), \(f(x)\leq g(x)\)
Show that R is transitive

Take arbitrary \(x,y,z\in U\), assume \(xRy\) and \(yRz\)
\(xRy\implies f(x)\geq g(y)\)
\(yRz\implies f(y)\geq g(z)\)
Also, since \(f(x)\leq g(x)\) for all \(x\)
\(\implies g(y)\geq f(y)\)
\(\implies f(x)\geq g(y)\geq f(y)\geq g(z)\)
\(\implies f(x)\geq g(z)\)
\(\implies xRz\)

2015.F1.Q2, PS1.Q6
Let \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) be two (partial) orders in some space \(U\)
Let \(R_3\) be the relation defined by \(\big( x R_3 y \iff xR_1y \land xR_2y \big) \forall x,y \in U\)
Prove that \(R_3\) is a (partial) order

  1. Reflective
    Since \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) are orders, \(xR_1 x\) and \(xR_2 x\) hold
    \(\implies xR_3x\)
  2. Transitive
    Since \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) are orders,
    \(xR_1 y\land yR_1 z\implies xR_1 z\)
    \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 z\implies xR_2 z\)
    Therefore,
    \(xR_3 y\land yR_3 z\) \(\implies\) \(xR_1 y\land yR_1z\) and \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 z\)
    \(\implies xR_1 z \land xR_2 z\)
    \(\implies xR_3 z\)
  3. Anti-Symmetry
    Since \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) are orders,
    \(xR_1 y\land yR_1 x\iff x=y\)
    \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 x\iff x=y\)
    Therefore,
    \(xR_3 y\land yR_3 x\) \(\iff\) \(xR_1 x\land yR_1 x\) and \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 x\)
    \(\iff x=y\)

Space

Distance

2014.F1.Q4
Let \(f: U\to R\) be injective
Define \(d(x, y)\equiv \big|f(x) - f(y)\big|\) for all \(x, y\in U\)
Show that \(d\) is a distance

  1. Non-negative (trivial)
  2. Identity of Indiscernibles
    Assume \(d(x, y) = 0\)
    \[\begin{aligned} |f(x) - f(y)| &= 0 \\ f(x) - f(y) &= 0 \\ f(x) &= f(y)\end{aligned}\] Since \(f\) is injective,
    \(f(x) = f(y) \implies x=y\)
  3. Symmetric
    For any \(x,y\in U\),
    \[\begin{aligned} d(x, y) &= \big|f(x) - f(y)\big|\\ &= \big|f(y) - f(x)\big| \\ &= d(y,x)\end{aligned}\]
  4. Triangle Inequality
    For any \(x,y,z\in U\),
    \[\begin{aligned} d(x,z) &= \big|f(x) - f(z)\big| \\ &= \big|f(x) - f(y) + f(y) - f(z)\big| \\ &\leq \big|f(x) - f(y)\big| + \big|f(y) - f(z)\big| \\ &= d(x,y) + d(y,z)\end{aligned}\]

Set Collection & Operation

Proof by Deduction

2014.F1.Q1
Let \(\{A_n \ | \ n\in N\}\) be a collection of sets, and \(B\) a set, all in some universe \(U\)
Show that \(B\cap \big( \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\big)\) \(=\) \(\cup_{n=1}^{\infty}\big( B \cap A_n\big)\)

  1. (\(\subset\))
    Take \(x\in B\cap \big( \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\big)\)
    \(\implies x\in B\) and \(x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\)
    \(\implies x\in A_{n_0}\) for some \(n_0\)
    \(\implies x\in B\cap A_{n_0}\)
    \(\implies x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty}\big( B \cap A_n\big)\)
  2. (\(\supset\))
    Take \(x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty}\big( B \cap A_n\big)\)
    \(\implies\) exists some \(n_0\) s.t. \(x\in B\cap A_{n_0}\)
    \(\implies x\in B\) and \(x\in B\cap A_{n_0}\)
    \(\implies x\in B\) and \(x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\)
    \(\implies x\in B\cap \big( \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\big)\)

Proof by \(B_ε\)

2015.F1.Q1
Fix \(x_0\in \mathbb{R}^n\)
Show that \(\{x_0\} =\cap_{ε\in \mathbb{R}_{++}} B_ε(x_0)\) for each \(ε>0\)
where, \(B_ε(x_0)\) is the open ball of radius \(ε\) centered at \(x_0\)

Since \(x_0 \in B_ε(x_0)\) for all \(ε>0\)
\(\implies x_0\in \cap_{ε\in \mathbb{R}_{++}} B_ε(x_0)\)
FSOC assume exists \(x'\neq x_0\), \(x'\in \cap_{ε\in \mathbb{R}_{++}} B_ε(x_0)\)
\(\implies x'\in B_ε(x_0)\) for all \(ε>0\)
\(\implies x'\in B_{d(x_0,x')}(x_0)\), contradiction

Close / Open

2014.F1.Q3
Let \(\langle U,d\rangle\) be a metric space, \(x^* \in U\) an element, \(r > 0\) a real number
Prove that \(B_r(x^*) =\{x\in U \ | \ d(x,x^*) < r\}\) is an open set

Take arbitrary \(x\in B_r(x^*)\), let \(ε = r - d(x,x^*)\)
For any \(x' \in B_ε(x)\),
\[\begin{aligned} d(x',x^*)&\leq d(x,x^*) + d(x',x) \\ &< d(x,x^*) + ε \\ &= d(x,x^*) + r - d(x,x^*) \\ &= r\end{aligned}\] \(\implies x'\in B_r(x^*)\)
\(\implies B_r(x^*)\) is open

Convex

2014.F1.Q9
Let \(A\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) be a set
Prove that there exists a set \(B\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) s.t. \(B\) is convex, \(A\subset B\)
and \(B\subset B'\) for all convex sets \(B'\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) such that \(A\subset B'\)

Define \(B\equiv \{ x \ | \ x = λa + (1-λ)b\}\) for all \(a,b\in A\) and \(λ\in[0,1]\)
\(B\) is convex by construction
Since \(\begin{cases} x=a & \text{when } λ=1 \\ x=b & \text{when } λ=0\end{cases}\),
\(\implies A\subset B\)
FSOC assume for some convex set \(B'\) s.t. \(A\subset B'\), \(B\not\subset B'\)
\(\implies\) exists \(a,b\in A\) and \(λ\) s.t.
\[\begin{cases} λa + (1-λ)b &\in B' \\ λa + (1-λ)b &\not\in B\end{cases}\] Contradiction.

Contraction

2014.F1.Q7
Assume that \(f, g: \mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}\) are contractions with \(λ_f\) and \(λ_g\)
Show that \(h\equiv f\circ g\) is a contraction

Take arbitrary \(x,x'\in \mathbb{R}\)
\[\begin{aligned} d\big(h(x), h(x')\big) &= d\Big(f\big(g(x)\big), f\big(g(x')\big)\Big) \\ &\leq λ_f\cdot d\big( g(x), g(x')\big) \\ &\leq λ_f\cdot \big(λ_g \cdot d(x,x')\big) \\ &= (λ_f\cdot λ_g) \cdot d(x,x')\end{aligned}\] Since \(λ_f,λ_g\in [0,1)\),
\(\implies λ_f\cdot λ_g \in [0,1)\)
\(\implies h\) is a contraction

Supremum / Infimum

2014.F1.Q8, PS4.Q7
Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}\) be bounded
Assume for all \(x\in \mathbb{R}\), \(f(x,y)\) is increasing in \(y\)
Show that \(V(y)\equiv\sup_{x\in\mathbb{R}} f(x,y)\) is increasing in \(y\)

\(f\) is bounded \(\implies \{ f(x) | x\geq y \}\) is bounded
\(\implies V(y)\) is well-defined
Take arbitrary \(y_1\leq y_2 \in \mathbb{R}\)
Since \(f\) is increasing in \(y\),
\(\implies f(x,y_1)\leq f(x,y_2)\) for all \(x\)
\(V(y_2) = \sup_{x\in\mathbb{R}} f(x,y_2)\)
\(\implies V(y_2)\) is the least upper bound of \(f(x,y_2)\)
\(\implies V(y_2)\) is an upper bound of \(f(x,y_1)\)
And \(V(y_1) = \sup_{x\in\mathbb{R}} f(x,y_1)\)
\(\implies V(y_1)\) is the least upper bound of \(f(x,y_1)\)
\(\implies V(y_1)\leq V(y_2)\)

Concave & Convex

2014.F1.Q5, PS7.Q7
Let \(f: \mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}\) be strictly convex
Prove that \(f\) has at most 2 zeros
(that is, set \(A\equiv\{x \in\mathbb{R} \ | \ f(x) = 0\}\) contains at most 2 elements

FSOC assume \(f(x) = 0\) has more than two solutions
\(\implies\) Exist \(x<y<z\in\mathbb{R}\) s.t. \(f(x) = f(y) = f(z) = 0\)
Let \(λ = \frac{z-y}{z-x}\), \(y = λx + (1-λ)z\)
Since \(f\) is strictly convex,
\[\begin{aligned} f\big(λx + (1-λ)z\big) &< λf(x) + (1-λ)f(z) \\ f(y) &< λf(x) + (1-λ)f(z) \\ 0 &< 0+0\end{aligned}\] Contradiction.

2014.F1.Q6, PS7.Q3
Prove that if \(f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\) is convex,
then \(\frac{f(y)-f(x)}{y-x}\) is increasing for all \(y > x\)

Take \(x < y_1 \leq y_2\)
Take \(λ\) s.t.
\[\begin{aligned} y_1 &= λx + (1-λ)y_2 \\ 1-λ &= \frac{y_1 - x}{y_2 - x} \\ λ &= \frac{y_2-y_1}{y_2 - x}\end{aligned}\] Since \(f\) is convex, \[\begin{aligned} f(y_1) &\leq λf(x) + (1-λ)f(y_2) \\ f(y_1) - f(x) &\leq λf(x) -f(x) + (1-λ) f(y_2) \\ &= -(1-λ) f(x) + (1-λ) f(y_2) \\ &= \frac{y_1 - x}{y_2 - x}\cdot \big(f(y_2) - f(x)\big) \\ \frac{f(y_1)-f(x)}{y_1 - x} &\leq \frac{f(y_2)-f(x)}{y_2-x}\end{aligned}\]
