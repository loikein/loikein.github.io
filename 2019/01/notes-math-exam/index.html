Relation

2014.F1.Q2
Let \(R\) be a relation on some set \(U\)
Assume that there exist two functions \(f,g: U\to \mathbb{R}\)
For all \(x,y\in U\), \(xRy\) \(\iff\) \(f(x)\geq g(y)\)
For all \(x\in U\), \(f(x)\leq g(x)\)
Show that R is transitive

Take arbitrary \(x,y,z\in U\), assume \(xRy\) and \(yRz\)
\(xRy\implies f(x)\geq g(y)\)
\(yRz\implies f(y)\geq g(z)\)
Also, since \(f(x)\leq g(x)\) for all \(x\)
\(\implies g(y)\geq f(y)\)
\(\implies f(x)\geq g(y)\geq f(y)\geq g(z)\)
\(\implies f(x)\geq g(z)\)
\(\implies xRz\)

2015.F1.Q2, PS1.Q6
Let \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) be two (partial) orders in some space \(U\)
Let \(R_3\) be the relation defined by \(\big( x R_3 y \iff xR_1y \land xR_2y \big) \forall x,y \in U\)
Prove that \(R_3\) is a (partial) order

  1. Reflective
    Since \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) are orders, \(xR_1 x\) and \(xR_2 x\) hold
    \(\implies xR_3x\)
  2. Transitive
    Since \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) are orders,
    \(xR_1 y\land yR_1 z\implies xR_1 z\)
    \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 z\implies xR_2 z\)
    Therefore,
    \(xR_3 y\land yR_3 z\) \(\implies\) \(xR_1 y\land yR_1z\) and \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 z\)
    \(\implies xR_1 z \land xR_2 z\)
    \(\implies xR_3 z\)
  3. Anti-Symmetry
    Since \(R_1\) and \(R_2\) are orders,
    \(xR_1 y\land yR_1 x\iff x=y\)
    \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 x\iff x=y\)
    Therefore,
    \(xR_3 y\land yR_3 x\) \(\iff\) \(xR_1 x\land yR_1 x\) and \(xR_2 y\land yR_2 x\)
    \(\iff x=y\)

2016.F1.Q2
Let \(U\) be a set and \(2^U\) its power set
Show that the inclusion \(\subset\) is a partial order in \(2^U\)
Remember that \(A\subset B\) \(\iff\) for all \(x\in U\), (\(x\in A\) implies \(x\in B\))

  1. Reflective
    Take arbitrary \(A\in 2^U\)
    \(x\in A \implies x\in A\)
    \(\implies A\subset A\)
  2. Transitive
    Take \(A, B, C\in 2^U\) s.t. \(A\subset B\) and \(B\subset C\)
    \(A\subset B\implies\) \((x\in A \implies x\in B)\)
    \(B\subset C\implies\) \((x\in B \implies x\in C)\)
    \(\implies (x\in A \implies x\in B\implies x\in C)\)
    \(\implies (x\in A \implies x\in C)\)
    \(\implies A\subset C\)
  3. Anti-Symmetry
    Take \(A, B\in 2^U\) s.t. \(A\subset B\) and \(B\subset A\)
    \(A\subset B\implies\) \((x\in A \implies x\in B)\)
    \(B\subset A\implies\) \((x\in B \implies x\in A)\)
    \(\implies x\in A\iff x\in B\)
    \(\iff A = B\)

Distance

2014.F1.Q4
Let \(f: U\to R\) be injective
Define \(d(x, y)\equiv \big|f(x) - f(y)\big|\) for all \(x, y\in U\)
Show that \(d\) is a distance

  1. Non-negative (trivial)
  2. Identity of Indiscernibles
    Assume \(d(x, y) = 0\)
    \[\begin{aligned} |f(x) - f(y)| &= 0 \\ f(x) - f(y) &= 0 \\ f(x) &= f(y)\end{aligned}\] Since \(f\) is injective,
    \(f(x) = f(y) \implies x=y\)
  3. Symmetric
    For any \(x,y\in U\),
    \[\begin{aligned} d(x, y) &= \big|f(x) - f(y)\big|\\ &= \big|f(y) - f(x)\big| \\ &= d(y,x)\end{aligned}\]
  4. Triangle Inequality
    For any \(x,y,z\in U\),
    \[\begin{aligned} d(x,z) &= \big|f(x) - f(z)\big| \\ &= \big|f(x) - f(y) + f(y) - f(z)\big| \\ &\leq \big|f(x) - f(y)\big| + \big|f(y) - f(z)\big| \\ &= d(x,y) + d(y,z)\end{aligned}\]

2015.F1.Q9
Let \(f: \mathbb{R}_+ \to \mathbb{R}\) be concave, strictly increasing, and \(f(0) = 0\)
Prove that \(d(x, y) \equiv f\big(|x − y|\big)\) for all \(x, y\in \mathbb{R}\) is a distance

  1. Non-negative
    Since \(f\) is strictly increasing, for all \(x, y\in \mathbb{R}\),
    \[\begin{aligned} |x − y|&\geq 0 \\ f\big(|x − y|\big)&\geq f(0) \\ d(x,y)&\geq 0\end{aligned}\]
  2. Identity of Indiscernibles
    For any \(x\in \mathbb{R}\),
    \[\begin{aligned} d(x,x) &= f\big(|x − x|\big) \\ &= f(0) = 0\end{aligned}\]
  3. Symmetric
    For all \(x, y\in \mathbb{R}\),
    \[\begin{aligned} d(x,y) &= f\big(|x − y|\big) \\ &= f\big(|y − x|\big) \\ &= d(y,x)\end{aligned}\]
  4. Triangle Inequality
    For arbitrary \(x\leq y\in \mathbb{R}\), consider any \(z\in \mathbb{R}\)
    1. \(x\leq z\leq y\)
      Take \(λ\) s.t.
      \[\begin{aligned} z &= λx + (1-λ)y \\ 1-λ &= \frac{z-x}{y-x} \\ λ &= \frac{y-z}{y-x}\end{aligned}\] Therefore, \(y-z = λ(y-x) + (1-λ) \cdot 0\)
      Since \(f\) is concave,
      \[\begin{aligned} d(y,z) &= f(|y-z|)\\ &= f(y-z)\\ &\geq λf(y-x) + (1-λ)f(0)\\ &= λf(y-x)\end{aligned}\] Similarly, \(z-x = (1-λ)(y-x) + λ \cdot 0\)
      \[\begin{aligned} d(z,x) &= f(|z-x|)\\ &= f(z-x)\\ &\geq (1-λ)f(y-x) + λf(0)\\ &= (1-λ)f(y-x)\end{aligned}\] \[\begin{aligned} d(y,z) + d(z,x) &\geq λf(y-x) + (1-λ)f(y-x)\\ &=f(y-x) = f(|y-x|)\\ &= d(y,x)\end{aligned}\]
    2. \(z < x\leq y\)
      \(\implies |y-z| > |x-z|\)
      Since \(f\) is strictly increasing,
      \[\begin{aligned} f(|y-z|) &> f(|x-z|) \\ d(y,z) &> d(x,z) \\ d(y,z) + d(y,x) &> d(x,z)\end{aligned}\]
    3. \(x\leq y < z\)
      \(\implies |z-y| \geq |z-x|\)
      Since \(f\) is increasing,
      \[\begin{aligned} f(|z-y|) &\geq f(|z-x|) \\ d(z,y) &\geq d(z,x) \\ d(z,y) + d(y,x) &> d(z,x)\end{aligned}\]

Proof by Deduction

2014.F1.Q1
Let \(\{A_n \ | \ n\in N\}\) be a collection of sets, and \(B\) a set, all in some universe \(U\)
Show that \(B\cap \big( \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\big)\) \(=\) \(\cup_{n=1}^{\infty}\big( B \cap A_n\big)\)

  1. (\(\subset\))
    Take \(x\in B\cap \big( \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\big)\)
    \(\implies x\in B\) and \(x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\)
    \(\implies x\in A_{n_0}\) for some \(n_0\)
    \(\implies x\in B\cap A_{n_0}\)
    \(\implies x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty}\big( B \cap A_n\big)\)
  2. (\(\supset\))
    Take \(x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty}\big( B \cap A_n\big)\)
    \(\implies\) exists some \(n_0\) s.t. \(x\in B\cap A_{n_0}\)
    \(\implies x\in B\) and \(x\in B\cap A_{n_0}\)
    \(\implies x\in B\) and \(x\in \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\)
    \(\implies x\in B\cap \big( \cup_{n=1}^{\infty} A_n\big)\)

2016.F1.Q1, PS1.Q4
Let \(A, B \subset U\) be two sets
Prove that \((A\backslash B)^c = A^c \cup B\)

Let \(x\in (A\backslash B)^c\)
\(\iff \lnot (x \in A \backslash B)\)
\(\iff \lnot (x \in A \land x\notin B)\)
\(\iff (x \notin A) \lor (x\in B)\)
\(\iff (x \in A^c) \lor (x\in B)\)
\(\iff x \in A^c \cup B\)
\(\implies (A\backslash B)^c = A^c \cup B\)

Proof by \(B_ε\)

2015.F1.Q1
Fix \(x_0\in \mathbb{R}^n\)
Show that \(\{x_0\} =\cap_{ε\in \mathbb{R}_{++}} B_ε(x_0)\) for each \(ε>0\)
where, \(B_ε(x_0)\) is the open ball of radius \(ε\) centered at \(x_0\)

Since \(x_0 \in B_ε(x_0)\) for all \(ε>0\)
\(\implies x_0\in \cap_{ε\in \mathbb{R}_{++}} B_ε(x_0)\)
FSOC assume exists \(x'\neq x_0\), \(x'\in \cap_{ε\in \mathbb{R}_{++}} B_ε(x_0)\)
\(\implies x'\in B_ε(x_0)\) for all \(ε>0\)
\(\implies x'\in B_{d(x_0,x')}(x_0)\), contradiction

Close / Open

2014.F1.Q3
Let \(\langle U,d\rangle\) be a metric space, \(x^* \in U\) an element, \(r > 0\) a real number
Prove that \(B_r(x^*) =\{x\in U \ | \ d(x,x^*) < r\}\) is an open set

Take arbitrary \(x\in B_r(x^*)\), let \(ε = r - d(x,x^*)\)
For any \(x' \in B_ε(x)\),
\[\begin{aligned} d(x',x^*)&\leq d(x,x^*) + d(x',x) \\ &< d(x,x^*) + ε \\ &= d(x,x^*) + r - d(x,x^*) \\ &= r\end{aligned}\] \(\implies x'\in B_r(x^*)\)
\(\implies B_r(x^*)\) is open

Convex

2015.F1.Q8, PS4.Q6
Let \(A, B \in \mathbb{R}^n\) be two convex sets
Let \(C \equiv \{ z\in \mathbb{R} | z=x+y, \ x\in A, \ y\in B \}\)
Prove that \(C\) is convex

Take some \(x_1, x_2 \in A\) and \(y_1,y_2 \in B\)
Take \(z_1, z_2 \in C\) s.t. \(z_1 = x_1+ y_1\), \(z_2 = x_2+ y_2\)
Let \[\begin{aligned} z_3 &\equiv λz_1 + (1-λ)z_2 \\ &= λ(x_1+y_1) + (1-λ)(x_2+y_2) \\ &= λx_1+(1-λ)x_2 + λy_1 + (1-λ)y_2 \end{aligned}\] Since \(A\) and \(B\) are convex
\(\implies λx_1+(1-λ)x_2 \in A\) and \(λy_1+(1-λ)y_2 \in B\)
\(\implies z_3 \in C\)
\(\implies C\) is convex

2014.F1.Q9
Let \(A\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) be a set
Prove that there exists a set \(B\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) s.t. \(B\) is convex, \(A\subset B\)
and \(B\subset B'\) for all convex sets \(B'\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) such that \(A\subset B'\)

Define \(B\equiv \{ x \ | \ x = λa + (1-λ)b\}\) for all \(a,b\in A\) and \(λ\in[0,1]\)
\(B\) is convex by construction
Since \(\begin{cases} x=a & \text{when } λ=1 \\ x=b & \text{when } λ=0\end{cases}\),
\(\implies A\subset B\)
FSOC assume for some convex set \(B'\) s.t. \(A\subset B'\), \(B\not\subset B'\)
\(\implies\) exists \(a,b\in A\) and \(λ\) s.t.
\[\begin{cases} λa + (1-λ)b &\in B' \\ λa + (1-λ)b &\not\in B\end{cases}\] Contradiction.

PS3.Q5, 2015.F1.Q4
Fix \(x\in \mathbb{R}^n\) and \(ε >0\), prove that \(B_ε(x)\) is convex
Hint: use \(d(x, y)\equiv \|x-y\|\) is a norm

Take arbitrary \(x_1, x_2 \in B_ε(x)\), \(λ\in (0,1)\)
\[\begin{aligned} d(y,x) &= d \big((λx_1+(1-λ)x_2),x\big) \\ &= \| λx_1 + (1-λ)x_2 - x \| \\ &= \| λx_1 + x_2 - λx_2 - x \| \\ &= \| λ (x_1-x) + (1-λ)(x_2-x) \| \\ &\leq \| λ (x_1-x) \| + \| (1-λ)(x_2-x) \| \\ &= λ\| x_1-x \| + (1-λ) \| x_2-x \| \\ &= λ d( x_1,x) + (1-λ) d( x_2,x ) \\ &< λε + (1-λ) ε \\ &= ε \end{aligned}\] \(\implies y \in B_ε(x)\)
\(\implies B_ε(x)\) is convex

Increasing / Decreasing

2015.F1. Q7
Let \(f\in\mathcal{C}^1(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})\)
Show that \(f\) is increasing \(\iff f'(x)\geq 0\) for all \(x\in\mathbb{R}\)
Hint: use the mean value theorem

  1. (\(\Longrightarrow\))
    FSOC assume \(f'(x)< 0\) for some \(x\)
    By mean value theorem, exists \(x_1\leq x_2\) s.t. \(x\in (x_1,x_2)\),
    \[\begin{aligned} f'(x) = \frac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{x_2 - x_1} &< 0 \\ f(x_2) - f(x_1) &< 0\end{aligned}\] Contradicts that \(f\) is increasing
  2. (\(\Longleftarrow\))
    FSOC assume \(f\) is not increasing
    Take arbitrary \(x_1\leq x_2\in\mathbb{R}\)
    By mean value theorem, exists \(x'\in (x_1,x_2)\) s.t.
    \[\begin{aligned} f'(x) = \frac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{x_2 - x_1} &\geq 0\\ f(x_2) - f(x_1) &\geq 0\end{aligned}\] Contradicts that \(f\) is not increasing

Value Theorems

2015.F1.Q6
Let \(f\in\mathcal{C}^1(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})\) be s.t. \(f'(x)\leq 1\) for all \(x\in\mathbb{R}\)
Prove that the set of fixed points of \(f\) is convex
Hint: use the mean value theorem

  1. The set is empty or singleton (trivial)
  2. The set contains more than 1 element
    Assume \(x_1,x_2\) are fixed points of \(f\)
    Let \(x_3 = λx_1 + (1-λ)x_2\)
    FSOC assume \(f(x_3)< x_3\)
    By mean value theorem, exists \(x'\in (x_3,x_2)\) s.t. \[\begin{aligned} f'(x') &= \frac{f(x_2) - f(x_3)}{x_2 - x_3} \\ &= \frac{x_2 - f(x_3)}{x_2 - x_3} \\ &= 1 + \frac{x_3 - f(x_3)}{x_2 - x_3} > 1\end{aligned}\] Contradiction.
    FSOC assume \(f(x_3) > x_3\)
    By mean value theorem, exists \(x'\in (x_1,x_3)\) s.t. \[\begin{aligned} f'(x') &= \frac{f(x_3) - f(x_1)}{x_3 - x_1} \\ &= \frac{f(x_3) - x_1}{x_3 - x_1} \\ &= 1 + \frac{f(x_3) - x_3}{x_3 - x_1} > 1\end{aligned}\] Contradiction.
    Therefore, \(f(x_3) = x_3\)
    \(\implies λx_1 + (1-λ)x_2\) is a fixed point

Contraction

2014.F1.Q7
Assume that \(f, g: \mathbb{R}\to \mathbb{R}\) are contractions with \(λ_f\) and \(λ_g\)
Show that \(h\equiv f\circ g\) is a contraction

Take arbitrary \(x,x'\in \mathbb{R}\)
\[\begin{aligned} d\big(h(x), h(x')\big) &= d\Big(f\big(g(x)\big), f\big(g(x')\big)\Big) \\ &\leq λ_f\cdot d\big( g(x), g(x')\big) \\ &\leq λ_f\cdot \big(λ_g \cdot d(x,x')\big) \\ &= (λ_f\cdot λ_g) \cdot d(x,x')\end{aligned}\] Since \(λ_f,λ_g\in [0,1)\),
\(\implies λ_f\cdot λ_g \in [0,1)\)
\(\implies h\) is a contraction

Supremum / Infimum

2014.F1.Q8, Let \(f:\mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}\) be bounded
Assume for all \(x\in \mathbb{R}\), \(f(x,y)\) is increasing in \(y\)
Show that \(V(y)\equiv\sup_{x\in\mathbb{R}} f(x,y)\) is increasing in \(y\)

\(f\) is bounded \(\implies \{ f(x) | x\geq y \}\) is bounded
\(\implies V(y)\) is well-defined
Take arbitrary \(y_1\leq y_2 \in \mathbb{R}\)
Since \(f\) is increasing in \(y\),
\(\implies f(x,y_1)\leq f(x,y_2)\) for all \(x\)
\(V(y_2) = \sup_{x\in\mathbb{R}} f(x,y_2)\)
\(\implies V(y_2)\) is the least upper bound of \(f(x,y_2)\)
\(\implies V(y_2)\) is an upper bound of \(f(x,y_1)\)
And \(V(y_1) = \sup_{x\in\mathbb{R}} f(x,y_1)\)
\(\implies V(y_1)\) is the least upper bound of \(f(x,y_1)\)
\(\implies V(y_1)\leq V(y_2)\)

2015.F1.Q3, PS4.Q7
Let \(f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\) be a bounded function and \(h(y) \equiv \sup_{x\geq y} \big( f(x) \big)\)
Show that \(h\) is decreasing

\(f\) is bounded \(\implies \{ f(x) | x\geq y \}\) is bounded
\(\implies h(\cdot)\) is well-defined
Take \(y_1\leq y_2\)
\(\implies \{ f(x)| x\geq y_2\} \subset \{ f(x)| x\geq y_1\}\)
Since \(h(y_1) = \sup_{x\geq y_1} \big( f(x) \big)\) is an upper bound of \(\{ f(x)| x\geq y_1\}\)
\(\implies h(y_1)\) is an upper bound of \(\{ f(x)| x\geq y_2\}\)
Since \(h(y_2)\) is the least upper bound of \(\{ f(x)| x\geq y_2\}\)
\(\implies h(y_1)\geq h(y_2)\)

2014.F1.Q5, PS7.Q7
Let \(f: \mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}\) be strictly convex
Prove that \(f\) has at most 2 zeros
(that is, set \(A\equiv\{x \in\mathbb{R} \ | \ f(x) = 0\}\) contains at most 2 elements

FSOC assume \(f(x) = 0\) has more than two solutions
\(\implies\) Exist \(x<y<z\in\mathbb{R}\) s.t. \(f(x) = f(y) = f(z) = 0\)
Take \(λ\) s.t.
\[\begin{aligned} y &= λx + (1-λ)z \\ λ &= \frac{z-y}{z-x}\end{aligned}\] Since \(f\) is strictly convex,
\[\begin{aligned} f\big(λx + (1-λ)z\big) &< λf(x) + (1-λ)f(z) \\ f(y) &< λf(x) + (1-λ)f(z) \\ 0 &< 0+0\end{aligned}\] Contradiction.

2014.F1.Q6, PS7.Q3
Prove that if \(f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\) is convex,
then \(\frac{f(y)-f(x)}{y-x}\) is increasing for all \(y > x\)

Take \(x < y_1 \leq y_2\)
Take \(λ\) s.t.
\[\begin{aligned} y_1 &= λx + (1-λ)y_2 \\ 1-λ &= \frac{y_1 - x}{y_2 - x} \\ λ &= \frac{y_2-y_1}{y_2 - x}\end{aligned}\] Since \(f\) is convex, \[\begin{aligned} f(y_1) &\leq λf(x) + (1-λ)f(y_2) \\ f(y_1) - f(x) &\leq λf(x) -f(x) + (1-λ) f(y_2) \\ &= -(1-λ) f(x) + (1-λ) f(y_2) \\ &= \frac{y_1 - x}{y_2 - x}\cdot \big(f(y_2) - f(x)\big) \\ \frac{f(y_1)-f(x)}{y_1 - x} &\leq \frac{f(y_2)-f(x)}{y_2-x}\end{aligned}\]

2015.F1.Q5, PS7.Q1
Let \(f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\) be strictly concave, \(x<y<z\in \mathbb{R}\)
Prove that \(\frac{f(y)-f(x)}{y-x}>\frac{f(z)-f(x)}{z-x}>\frac{f(z)-f(y)}{z-y}\)

Take \(λ\) s.t. \[\begin{aligned} y &= λ x+(1-λ)z \\ y -x &= -(1-λ)x + (1-λ)z \\ &= (1-λ)(z-x) \\ 1-λ &= \frac{y-x}{z-x} \\ λ &= \frac{z-y}{z-x}\end{aligned}\] Since \(f\) is strictly concave,
\[\begin{aligned} f(y) &> λf(x) + (1-λ)f(z) \\ f(y) - f(x) &> λf(x) - f(x) + (1-λ) f(z) \\ &= (1-λ) \big( f(z) - f(x)\big) \\ &= \frac{y-x}{z-x} \big(f(z) -f(x)\big) \\ \frac{f(y)-f(x)}{y-x} &> \frac{f(z)-f(x)}{z-x}\end{aligned}\] Similarly,
\[\begin{aligned} f(y) &> λf(x) + (1-λ)f(z) \\ f(y)-f(z) &> λf(x) + (1-λ) f(z) - f(z) \\ &= λ \big(f(x) - f(z)\big) \\ f(z) - f(y) &< λ\big(f(z)- f(x)\big) \\ &= \frac{z-y}{z-x}\big(f(z) - f(x)\big) \\ \frac{f(z)-f(y)}{z-y} &< \frac{f(z)-f(x)}{z-x}\end{aligned}\]

Convergent

2016.F1.Q3
Let \(f: \mathbb{R}^m \to \mathbb{R}^m\) be a continuous function
Fix \(x_1\in\mathbb{R}^m\) and let \((x_n)_n\) be the sequence generated by \(x_{n+1} = f(x_n)\) for all \(n\in \mathbb{N}\)
Prove that if \(x_n \to x^*\) for some \(x^*\in\mathbb{R}^m\),
then \(x^*\) is a steady state of the recurrence relation (\(f(x^*) = x^*\))

Let \((x_n)_n \to x^*\)
\(\implies (x_{n+1})_n \to x^*\)
\(\implies \big(f(x_n)\big)_n \to x^*\)
Since \(f\) is continuous, \(\big(f(x_n)\big)_n\to f(x^*)\)
\(\implies f(x^*) = x^*\)
