Basic Ideas

Firm

  • Firm: transforms inputs into outputs
  • Profit: \(Π = R(q) - C(q)\)
  • Sales: \(q = f(k,l)\)
  • Cost: technology, expenditure minimization
    Let \(q = \tilde{q}\), isovalue of production: \(f(k,l) = \tilde{q}\)
    Let \(p_k\) be price of k, \(p_l\) be price of l, given \(E\), isovalue of expenditure: \(k = -\frac{p_l}{p_k}\cdot l + \frac{E}{p_k}\)
    Therefore, cost: \(c(\tilde{q})=p_k\cdot\tilde{k} + p_l\cdot\tilde{l}\)
  • Revenue: demand, market interaction

Consumer

  • Income: \(I\)
  • Demand: \(Q(p)\)
    • Individual demand: \(Q_i(p_i, p_{-i}, T)\)
    • Shift in \(p_i\) moves along the curve, shift in other variables moves the curve
  • Inverse demand: \(P(q) = Q^{-1}(p)\)

Discrete Choice Model

  • \(M\) consumers, unit demand
  • Willingness to pay: \(v_i\) (known)
    • \(v_1\geq v_2\geq\dots\)
  • Consumer’s utility: \(u_i(p)=\begin{cases}v_i-p &\text{buy} \\ 0 &\text{no buy}\end{cases}\)
    • Buy \(\iff v_i\geq p\)
  • Demand: \[Q(p) = \max_\{i \text{ s.t. } v_i\geq p\}\]
    • Decreasing in \(p\)

Discrete Choice Model: Asymmetric Information

  • cdf of \(v\): \(F(v)\)
    • \(v\in[\underline{v},\bar{v}]\)
  • Expected demand: \[\begin{aligned}Q(p) &= M\cdot Pr(v\geq p) \\ &= M\cdot\big(1-F(p)\big)\end{aligned}\]
    • Decreasing in \(p\)
    • When \(F(v)\) is uniform, \(Q(p)\) is linear

Representative Consumer Model

  • Identical consumers
  • Consumer’s utility: \[U(q_0, q_1,\dots, q_I) &= q_0 + u_1(q_1) + u_2(q_2) +\dots+u_I(q_I)\]
    • Goods: \(i\in\{1,\dots, I\}\)
    • Money left (Hicksian composite good): \(q_0\), \(p_0=1\)
    • Concave utility function: \(u_i\)
  • Each consumer’s problem: \[\begin{aligned}\max_{q_i\geq 0} && &\phantom{\ggg}U(q_0, q_1,\dots, q_I)\\ && &= q_0 + u_1(q_1) + u_2(q_2) +\dots+u_I(q_I) \\ \text{s.t.} && &q_0 + p_1q_1 + p_2q_2 + \dots + p_Iq_I \leq I\end{aligned}\] Lagrangian: \[\begin{aligned}\mathcal{L} &= q_0 + u_1(q_1) + u_2(q_2) +\dots+u_I(q_I)\\ &\phantom{\ggg}-λ(q_0 + p_1q_1 + p_2q_2 + \dots + p_Iq_I - I)\end{aligned}\] FOC: \[\begin{aligned}\frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial q_0} &= 1-λ \overset{!}{=} 0\\ λ &= 1 \\ \frac{\partial\mathcal{L}}{\partial q_i} &= u_i'(q_i) - λ\cdot p_i \overset{!}{=} 0\\ u_i'(q_i) &= p_i \\ q_i &= (u_i')^{-1}(p_i) \\ p_i(q_i)&= u_i'(q_i)\end{aligned}\]
    • \(q_i\) is increasing in \(p_i\) if \(u''<0\)

Welfare

  • Partial equilibrium
    • Assume 1: change in \(p_i\) does not affect \(q_{-i}\)
    • Assume 2: no serious substitution effects
  • Measure
    • Firm: profit
    • Consumer: surplus (triangle)
    • Walfare = profit + consumer surplus
  • Discrete choice: \(u_i(p)=\begin{cases}v_i-p &\text{buy} \\ 0 &\text{no buy}\end{cases}\) \[\begin{aligned}CS &= \sum_{i=1}^m u_i(\tilde{p}) \\ &= \int_{\tilde{p}}^{\bar{p}} Q(p)\, dp\end{aligned}\]
  • Representative consumer: \(p_i(q_i)= u_i'(q_i)\) \[\begin{aligned}CS &= \int_{0}^{Q_i(\tilde{p}_i)} P(q_i) - \tilde{p}_i\, dq_i \\ &= \int_{0}^{Q_i(\tilde{p}_i)} u_i'(q_i) - \tilde{p}_i\, dq_i \\ &= \big[ u_i(q_i) - \tilde{p}_i\cdot q_i \big]_{0}^{Q_i(\tilde{p}_i)} \\ &= u_i\big(Q_i(\tilde{p}_i)\big) - \tilde{p}_i\cdot Q_i(\tilde{p}_i)\end{aligned}\]
  • Social welfare: \[\begin{aligned}\max_p && W&= p\cdot Q(p) - C\big(Q(p)\big) + \int_p^{\bar{p}} Q(s)\, ds \\ FOC && \frac{\partial W}{\partial p} &= Q(p) + p\cdot Q'(p) - C'\big(Q(p)\big)\cdot Q'(p) - Q(p) \\ && p^* &= C'\big(Q(p^*)\big) \end{aligned}\] price = marginal cost
