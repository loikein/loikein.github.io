Behavioural Public Policy

Departures from the standard model:

  • Present bias: can not correctly understand future self
  • Salience bias: can only notice the most obvious thing
  • Non-selfish preferences: contribute to public goods

which lead to:

  • Create scope for policy intervention (?)
  • Worsen market failures
  • Reduce market failures

Theory: Intertemporal Consumption

  • Utility discounting: \[U = u(c_t) + \beta \big[\delta u(c_{t+1}) + \delta^2 u(c_{t+2}) + \delta^3 u(c_{t+3}) + \cdots + \delta^T u(c_{t+T})\big]\]
    • Standard model: \(\beta=1\)
    • Present bias: \(\beta<1\)
  • Budget constraint (can borrow): \[c_t + c_{t+1} + \dots + c_{t+T} \leq y_t + y_{t+1} + \dots + y_{t+T}\]
    • Total available assets: \[\begin{aligned}a_t &= y_t + y_{t+1} + \dots + y_{t+T} \\ a_{t+1} &= a_t - c_t\end{aligned}\]
  • Bellman equation: for all \(s = t, t+1,\dots, t+T\) \[\begin{aligned}V_t(a_t) &= \max_{c_t} \ u(c_t) + \delta\cdot V_{t+1}(a_t - c_t) \\ &= \max_{c_t} \ u(c_t) + \delta\cdot \underbrace{ u\big(c_{t+1}(a_t - c_t)\big) +\delta\cdot V_{t+2}\big(a_t - c_t - c_{t+1}(a_t - c_t)\big)}_{V_{t+1}(a_t - c_t)}\end{aligned}\] Therefore, FOC: \[\begin{aligned}u'(c_s) &= \delta \cdot V'_{s+1}(a_s - c_s) \\ &= \delta \cdot u'\big(c_{s+1}(a_s - c_s)\big) \cdot \frac{\partial c_{s+1}}{\partial a} \\ &\phantom{\ggg}+\delta^2\cdot V'_{s+2}\big(a_t - c_t - c_{s+1}(a_s - c_s)\big) \cdot \Big(1-\frac{\partial c_{s+1}}{\partial a}\Big)\\ u'(c_{s+1}) &= \delta\cdot V'_{s+2}(a_{s+1} - c_{s+1}) \\ u'(c_s) &= \delta\cdot u'\big(c_{s+1}(a_s - c_s)\big) \cdot \frac{\partial c_{s+1}}{\partial a} \\ &\phantom{\ggg}+ \delta\cdot u'\big(c_{s+1}(a_s - c_s)\big) \cdot \big(1 -\frac{\partial c_{s+1}}{\partial a}\big)\\ &= \delta\cdot u'\big(c_{s+1}(a_s - c_s)\big) \end{aligned}\] \(\implies\) Euler equation

Agents

  • TC
    • \(\beta=1\)
    • Set consumption s.t. \[u'(c_t^{TC}) = \delta\cdot u'(c_{t+1}^{TC})\]
  • Naif
    • \(\beta <1\), but think themselves will behave like TC in the future
    • \(\implies\) can generate consumption decline over short period
    • Set consumption s.t. \[u'(c_t^{N}) = \beta\delta\cdot u'(c_{t+1}^{N})\]
  • Sophisticate
    • \(\beta <1\), and they know it
    • Set consumption s.t. \[u'(c_t^S) = u'(c_{t+1}^S)\cdot\Big(\beta\delta\cdot\frac{\delta c_{t+1}^S}{\delta a}+\delta\big(1-\frac{\delta c_{t+1}^S}{\delta a}\big)\Big)\]
    • If \(\frac{\delta c_{t+1}^S}{\delta a}\approx 1\), no motive to save
    • If \(\frac{\delta c_{t+1}^S}{\delta a}\ll 1\), save & more patient

Augenblick, Niederle, Sprenger (2015)

  • Procrastination & present bias in work

Experiment

  • Tedious work for two sessions
    • \(t=0\): initial allocation: \(e_1\) and \(e_2\)
    • \(t=1\): re-allocation; randomly pick between the allocations; session 1
    • \(t=2\): session 2
  • Notations
    • \(R\): interest rate
    • \(B\): basic income
    • \(e_1\) and \(e_2\): effort costs
    • \(e_1 + R e_2 = 50\): budget constraint
  • \(t=0\) \[\begin{aligned}\max_{e_1^0,e_2^0} && U_0 &= \beta\delta^2 B - \beta (\delta c(e_1) + \delta^2 c(e_2))\\ FOC && c'(e_1) &= \frac{\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2)\end{aligned}\]
    • \(\frac{\delta}{R}=1\implies e_1=e_2\)
    • \(\frac{\delta}{R}<1\implies e_1<e_2\)
    • \(\frac{\delta}{R}>1\implies e_1>e_2\)
  • \(t=1\) \[\begin{aligned}\max_{e_1^1,e_2^1} && U_1 &= \beta\delta B - (c(e_1) + \beta\delta c(e_2))\\ FOC && c'(e_1) &= \frac{\beta\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2) < \frac{\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2)\end{aligned}\]
    • \(\implies e_1^1<e_1^0\), \(e_2^1>e_2^0\)
    • 5% - 10% procrastination
    • Is that a lot?

Calibration (Specifying the Model by Guessing)

  • Assume \(c(e)=0.5\cdot c\cdot e^2\)
    • \(c'(e) = ce\)
  • When \(t=0\), FOC: \[\begin{aligned}c'(e_1^0) &= \frac{\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2^0)\\ ce_1^0 &= \frac{\delta}{R}\cdot ce_2^0 \\ \implies \delta &= R\cdot\frac{e_1^0}{e_2^0}\end{aligned}\]
  • When \(t=1\), FOC: \[\begin{aligned}c'(e_1^1) &= \frac{\beta\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2^1) \\ ce_1^1 &= \frac{\beta\delta}{R}\cdot ce_2^1 \\ \implies \beta\delta &= R\cdot\frac{e_1^1}{e_2^1}\end{aligned}\]
  • Therefore, present bias: \[\beta = \frac{e_1^1\ /\ e_2^1}{e_1^0\ /\ e_2^0}\]

Structual Estimation (Better Guessing with Generalisation)

  • Assume \(c(e)=\frac{1}{\gamma}\cdot c\cdot e^{\gamma}\)
    • \(\gamma\geq 1\)
  • When \(t=0\), FOC: \[\begin{aligned}c'(e_1^0) &= \frac{\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2^0)\\ c(e_1^0)^{\gamma -1} &= \frac{\delta}{R}\cdot c(e_2^0)^{\gamma -1} \end{aligned}\] take log of both sides: \[\begin{aligned}(\gamma -1)\cdot\log e_1^0 &= \log \delta - \log R + (\gamma -1)\cdot\log e_2^0 \\ \log e_2^0 - \log e_1^0 &= \frac{1}{\gamma -1}(- \log \delta + \log R) \end{aligned}\]
  • When \(t=1\), FOC: \[\begin{aligned}c'(e_1^1) &= \frac{\beta\delta}{R}\cdot c'(e_2^1) \\ c(e_1^1)^{\gamma -1} &= \frac{\beta\delta}{R}\cdot c(e_2^1)^{\gamma -1} \end{aligned}\] take log of both sides: \[\begin{aligned}(\gamma -1)\cdot\log e_1^1 &= \log \beta + \log \delta - \log R + (\gamma -1)\cdot\log e_2^1 \\ \log e_2^1 - \log e_1^1 &= \frac{1}{\gamma -1}(- \log \beta - \log \delta + \log R )\end{aligned}\]
  • \(\implies\) Econometrics model: \[\log e_{2ik}^t - \log e_{1ik}^t = \underbrace{\phi_0}_{\frac{-\log \delta}{\gamma -1}} +\underbrace{\phi_1}_{\frac{-\log \beta}{\gamma -1}}\cdot 1(t=1) +\underbrace{\phi_2}_{\frac{1}{\gamma -1}}\cdot \log R_k + \epsilon_{ikt}\]
    • \(k\): different values of \(R\)
    • \(\phi_2\): elasticity of effort wrt \(R\)
    • \(\delta = \exp\big(-\frac{\phi_0}{\phi_2}\big)\)
    • \(\beta = \exp\big(-\frac{\phi_1}{\phi_2}\big)\)

Andreoni, Callen, Khan, Jaffar, Sprenger (2016)

Field Experiment

  • Between-subject design
    • Advance choice group: \(e_1^0\), \(e_2^0\)
    • Immediate choice group: \(e_1^1\), \(e_2^1\)
  • Budget constraint: \(e_1 + R e_2 \geq 300\)
    • If actual work reaches \(e\), receive bonus

Round 1

  • For each value of \(R\), subjects have to choose \(e\)’s

Round 2 (same group of subjects)

  • Induce equalised effort over periods
    • Tailored group: set \(R\) s.t. \(R_i=\beta_i \cdot \delta_i\)
    • Untailored group: randomly assign \(R\)

Food Choice (Sadoff, Samek, Sprenger, 2014 & 2019)

  • 2014
  • 2019

Purpose:

  • Present-bias in food choice
  • Commitment tool
    • Self-control problems \(\implies\) unhealthy diet & high BMI
  • Strength of replication

Experiment

  • \(t=0\): pick item 1
  • \(t=1\): get item 1, pick 2
  • \(t=2\): get 2, pick 3
    • No commitment group: change item 2
  • \(t=3\): get 3
    • No commitment group: change item 3
  • Result: more consistent subjects tend to choose commitment
    • \(\implies\) stronger the bias, less aware of it
